About the Project

Building an AI
Action Series
from Scratch

This is Sari & Ploy, a short-form action series made entirely with AI video generation tools.

Two high school girls — one Indonesian, one Thai — secretly work as agents for a covert bilateral program called the Garuda-Siam Initiative. They fight crime across Southeast Asia using martial arts. Sari fights with Pencak Silat. Ploy fights with Muay Thai. In return for their service, the government takes care of everything — housing, education, career, financial security for their families.

The story is inspired by anime and manga. The idea of teenagers with extraordinary abilities living double lives is nothing new — Jujutsu Kaisen, Naruto, Spy x Family — but this project sets it in Southeast Asia, with characters that feel local and grounded.

Classified

The Garuda-Siam
Initiative

A classified joint program between the Indonesian and Thai governments. Formed in response to rising threats of organized crime operating across Southeast Asian borders. GSI operates completely off the books — no public records, no official acknowledgment.

GSI's mandate: recruit, train, and deploy unconventional assets to assist law enforcement operations in the region. They find individuals with abilities far beyond the ordinary. They give them a future. In return, those individuals give their talent.

To maintain cover, both agents are enrolled in a high school in Jakarta. Sari attends as a regular Indonesian student. Ploy is registered as an exchange student from Thailand. Their absences are managed by a GSI handler embedded as a teacher at the school.

How It's
Made

Every shot is generated individually. Detailed prompts are written for every single shot, specifying camera angles, lighting, action, and sound design. Everything is stitched together in post-production using Google's Nano Banana for consistent character references.

Visual consistency is maintained using omni-reference, and two main video models are used: Seedance 2.0 for cinematic output and Kling 3.0 for a candid aesthetic.

Ratu Sunyi // Southeast Asia // 2026

W
Character
Widuri
Tier 1 Operative / Ratu Sunyi

Her real name no longer exists. It was erased when she entered Ratu Sunyi — no record, no identity, no past. She was given the codename Widuri, a Javanese word for a lone star that guides sailors home. The handlers named her that because when they found her, she was the only fixed point in a room full of chaos. She had not moved in three days.

She was 12 when her father, a low-level court official, was murdered by a drug syndicate he refused to work with. A Ratu Sunyi handler found her still sitting beside his body. She had not eaten. She said nothing. She was already waiting.

Cold. Cynical. She has a gaze that mocks her opponents before they understand what is happening. Precise in execution. Privately ritualistic. She meditates before every contract, burns incense, watches TV static. She says it helps her listen to what the room wants. She is not cruel. She is surgical. There is a difference.

"A woman in kebaya is invisible to suspicion in Indonesian society. That is the weapon."

Ratu Sunyi — The Silent Queens — is an all-female assassin syndicate operating out of Southeast Asia. They recruit broken women and train them in pencak silat, bladed weapons, firearms, and poison. Their cover is the oldest cover in the world: domesticity. A woman at a wedding, at a funeral, at an arisan. Nobody looks twice.

The syndicate operates in the shadows of everyday life across Southeast Asia. Targets are exclusively drug lords, corrupt officials, and traffickers who buy their way out of justice. Ratu Sunyi is led by a founder known only as Ibu — never seen, only heard.

The city is always there in the background. Skylines. Wet pavement. A warung at 2AM. Fluorescent tubes in old buildings that never stop buzzing. This is not a clean world. It is a humid, neon-lit, deeply human world — and somewhere inside it, women in kebaya are doing things nobody will ever be able to explain.

About the Project
This started as a question: how far can AI go when you treat it like a camera?

Codename: Widuri is a micro-series shot entirely with generative AI. Every scene starts as a text-to-video prompt, written by hand, specific enough to function as a real shot list.

This project exists because omni-reference technology has been getting genuinely interesting. The ability to lock a character visually across scenes, same face, same costume, same presence, is what makes something like this possible. It is still far from perfect. Consistency in performance, location, props, and the smaller details remain a real challenge. But it is improving, and that is worth exploring seriously.

The character sheets used as omni-reference were generated with Google Nano Banana 2, chosen because their dataset carries stronger racial nuance for Southeast Asian faces. Text-to-video omni-reference is running on Kling 3.0.

Synopsis

A lone woman moves through a dead world. Temple spires and collapsed buildings drift under a pale grey sky. She carries one weapon. She asks no questions. Something finds her.

The world she walks through has no name she knows of. It does not age. It does not heal. The ruins of a civilization long fractured hang suspended in the air around her — caught between collapse and something that never came after.

She has no memory of before. She has a mission. That is enough. For now.

Statement

Generative AI video has made remarkable progress in a short amount of time. What once required entire production pipelines can now be explored by a single person with the right tools. The limitations are still real — consistency, physics, fine control — but the gap between idea and execution has never been smaller.

This project is an attempt to push that boundary — specifically around action sequences and micro storytelling. With Omni-Reference now available across most leading video models, character consistency across shots has become genuinely achievable. This pilot tests exactly that: how far the technology holds when the story demands movement, tension, and continuity.
