GLITCH IN THE ARCHIVE.

This isn't really a brand or a portfolio. It's more of a data leak?

Basically just trying to figure out what happens when u feed tropical humidity and urban loneliness into a neural net. I think the machine understands the feeling better than we do tbh.

01 // THE VIBE

Treating Jakarta and Bangkok not as cities, but as datasets. The traffic, the sweat, the school uniforms, the neon - it's all just training data for a feeling I can't articulate.

My operating system is fairly simple:

  • Humidity is the interface
  • Entropy is the goal
  • Fashion is armor for the simulation

It lives somewhere between a fever dream and a documentary that hasn't happened yet.

02 // THE MACHINE

I don't "direct" AI. I just vibe with it. We're jamming.

The machine hallucinates and I just capture the screenshots. It’s faster than thinking. It bypasses the logic centers and goes straight to the Lizard Brain.

Why write a script when you can just download the collective unconscious of the street?

  • Pre-verbal
  • Image-first
  • Slightly cursed but in a good way
03 // OBSESSIONS
01

Tropical Femininity

Just documenting how girls survive the heat and the density of SE-Asian megacities. It feels like a sci-fi movie that ran out of budget so they just filmed real life.

  • Candid moments
  • The way hair sticks to skin
  • Uniforms as camouflage
It's not the male gaze, it's the machine gaze.
02

Sensuality / Static

Desire is just another form of information transfer. I'm interested in the noise, the static, the things you aren't supposed to see.

  • Atmosphere > Narrative
  • Vintage exploitation aesthetics but modernized
  • The intensity of doing nothing in a hot room
Shock is boring. Atmosphere is forever.
03

The Urban Glitch

Jakarta and Bangkok are the main characters. They are messy, loud, and constantly overwriting themselves.

  • High-school-core
  • Retro ads that look haunted
  • Night markets
  • Liminal spaces
Nostalgia for a future that got cancelled.
04

Alt-Media / Artifacts

Screenshots, lo-fi renders, pixelated memories. If it looks too polished, I don't trust it. Real culture is low resolution.

  • Digital debris
  • Platform native formats
  • Things that disappear in 24 hours
Archiving the temporary.
04 // POSITION
I'm not trying to sell you the future.
I'm just trying to download the present before it crashes.

The Lab Prioritizes:

  • Vibes over Logic
  • Texture over Clarity
  • Instinct over Strategy
  • Chaos over Control

The Thesis:

Southeast Asia is already living in the cyberpunk future, it just looks messier than the movies.

I'm just here to record the glitches.

DAN VISUAL RESEARCHER / COPYWRITER
Jakarta based copywriter and visual researcher. Still working in the industry, just finding new ways to break the format.
[ THE EXPANSION ]

I still write copy. That's the day job. But words have limits. Sometimes the lizard brain ignores text and just reacts to heat and pattern.

MXVDXN is where I expand the toolkit. I'm not replacing the writing, I'm feeding it into a new engine. Using the instincts I sharpened in the ad world to direct generative systems. Watching how style and humidity mutate in the streets of Jakarta and Bangkok.

Copywriting is the logic. AI is the hallucination. I use both.

[ VISUAL SYSTEMS ]

We treat images like "content" but they are really just biological signals. My work is capturing the noise of daily life - street culture, bad TV, urban myths - and remixing it.

[ CHAOS ASSISTANT ]

I use AI to speed up the intuition process. It lets me prototype a vibe in seconds. Direction is still 100% human, but the machine helps me see the glitch faster. If it feels weird, it's working.
