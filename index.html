DAN PRADANA // MXVDXN

10+ years in Ad Copywriting // Current client: whatever my lizard brain wants // Gen AI from the lingering heat of Southeast Asia

Dan Pradana is a Jakarta-born copywriter who has worked across the APAC region. Generative AI is his favorite chaos assistant, fast, imperfect, and fun to argue with. He keeps Southeast Asian aesthetics at the core, because they deserve center stage, not side notes.

MXVDXN is Dan’s small independent AI lab, built to explore Southeast Asian subcultures through generative tools. Its focus sits somewhere between aesthetic research, remix culture, and creating new visual and narrative vibes that feel regionally grounded yet globally curious.

After more than ten years in advertising, he’s still obsessed with the rhythm of words and how they look on a screen. These days, he points that obsession toward the humid imagination of Southeast Asian subcultures, where lo-fi visuals, copywriting instinct, and internet weirdness collide. Think zine spirit meets post-agency peace of mind. Somewhere between iced kopi, karaoke nights, and fried rice at 2 a.m., ideas tend to show up.

His daily routine looks like a Southeast Asian lunch table: a bit messy, always loud, somehow works out in the end. Daily AI logs and tool experiments live on X. Visual dumps go to Instagram. Thai–Indo high school chaos sits on TikTok. A selected video portfolio archive lives on YouTube.

Playlist: Sabaicore / Rice-core Hip-Hop, a rotation of artists from SEA, China, South Korea, and Japan, basically countries that eat rice and drop bars. If you want to understand what fuels his world and vibe, check out his film list on Letterboxd — a window into the movies that shape his brand, still curating. No Hollywood stuff inside, promise.
